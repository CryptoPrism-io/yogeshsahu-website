Mental OS

How I approach hard problems.

The frameworks that shape how I diagnose, decide, and build. Not theory — applied daily in client work, product decisions, and system design.

01
First Principles
Break every problem to bedrock facts. Build up from there — not from how it's always been done.
Applied in client work
When a client says "we need X tool" I ask why — five times. Usually the real constraint is three layers deeper and half the cost.
Decision-Making
02
Inversion
"How would I guarantee this project fails?" Map those failure modes. Then systematically eliminate them.
Applied in client work
Before any architecture decision, I write the failure post-mortem first. It surfaces hidden risks before a line of code is written.
Risk & Systems
03
OODA Loop
Observe → Orient → Decide → Act — then loop faster than the competition. Tempo is the advantage.
Applied in client work
I structure sprints around the OODA loop. Faster internal decision cycles mean clients ship before competitors can respond.
Strategy
04
Antifragility
Don't just survive shocks — design systems that get stronger from them. Fragile is the real risk.
Applied in client work
CryptoPrism DB was designed to handle 10× normal load. That redundancy became a feature, not a cost, during market spikes.
Architecture
05
Circle of Competence
Know what you genuinely understand. Know precisely where that edge ends. Be explicit about both.
Applied in client work
I take fintech, data infrastructure, and AI mandates. I decline e-commerce and hardware. Saying no to the wrong mandate protects both parties.
Self-Awareness
06
Second-Order Thinking
First-order: what happens immediately. Second-order: what happens because of that. Keep going.
Applied in client work
Migrating to microservices looks efficient (1st order). Increases operational complexity and team cognitive load (2nd order). Often the wrong call for a 10-person team.
Systems Thinking
