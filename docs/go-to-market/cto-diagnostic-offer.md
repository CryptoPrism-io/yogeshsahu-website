# Solutions Architecture Diagnostic

## Positioning

For clients who need a hands-on chief solutions architect / fractional CTO to shape discovery, architecture, and delivery without committing to a full-time hire too early.

This is the paid entry offer. It is designed to create immediate clarity, stronger technical decision-making, and a practical 90-day execution path.

## Best Fit

- funded, regulated, or fast-moving teams
- fintech, data-heavy products, and AI-facing businesses
- companies where discovery, architecture, and delivery are not yet aligned
- businesses feeling pressure to integrate AI without a clear adoption path

## Common Triggers

- AI is on the agenda, but the path from experimentation to rollout is unclear
- architecture decisions are slowing execution instead of supporting it
- discovery is happening, but the scope and delivery plan are still soft
- the company needs CTO-level judgment, but a full-time hire feels too early or too rigid

## What Happens In 5 Days

### Day 1

Context intake: business goals, AI ambition, product pressure, technical landscape, stakeholder map, and discovery inputs.

### Day 2

Architecture review: current system shape, key tradeoffs, failure points, and cost-risk decisions.

### Day 3

Architecture and solution review: where AI belongs, what should remain lightweight, and what could become product or enterprise-grade.

### Day 4

Alignment review: product, engineering, and leadership friction, decision bottlenecks, and what should not be pursued.

### Day 5

Executive synthesis: decision memo, 90-day execution plan, and recommendation for the next operating cadence.

## Deliverables

- discovery summary and architecture decision memo
- delivery-risk assessment
- product-engineering alignment risks
- 90-day execution plan
- priorities across system, team, and stakeholder flow
- executive debrief

## Outcomes

By the end of the diagnostic, the client should know:

- where AI actually fits the business
- which architecture decisions matter now
- what is slowing execution
- where stakeholder alignment and delivery confidence are breaking down
- whether the next move is ongoing fractional CTO support, focused execution, or a hiring brief

Because the market moves quickly, and the wrong full-time technical hire is expensive to unwind.

Fractional leadership gives the company senior judgment, flexibility, and lower fixed-cost risk while the direction is still being shaped.
